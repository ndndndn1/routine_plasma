## Keyword match (>=2 required)
1. Microwave Interferometry — The diagnostic principle is broadband microwave reflection / interferometric measurement at a plasma boundary; the same wave-plasma interaction underpins both reflectometry and conventional interferometry, and the paper frames its analysis in microwave terms.
2. Inverse Problem Solving — The reflection coefficient |Gamma(f)| of an inhomogeneous slab is a non-linear functional of the unknown n_e(x), nu_en(x) profile. A neural network is trained to invert this functional from the *positions* of multiple resonant peaks in |Gamma(f)|.
3. Spatially Resolved Mapping — The output is a 1D profile n_e(x) (and nu_en(x)) across the plasma slab, not just a single line-averaged value, so the diagnostic resolves spatial gradients.
4. Plasma Sheath Dynamics — The methodology is motivated by, and demonstrated for, inhomogeneous sheath/boundary-layer plasmas where the density profile must be captured rather than averaged.

## Methods (summary)
- Physical model: a stratified-slab cold-plasma model with a prescribed family of inhomogeneous n_e(x) and collision frequency nu_en(x) profiles. The transfer-matrix / Riccati equation is solved across the slab to obtain the complex reflection coefficient Gamma(omega) over a microwave band (a few GHz to tens of GHz).
- Multi-peak feature: for inhomogeneous slabs, |Gamma(omega)| shows multiple local maxima (peaks) whose *positions* (omega_k) encode the n_e profile far more strongly than the peak amplitudes (which are sensitive to nu_en). Using peak positions as the primary feature gives a noise-robust, low-dimensional descriptor of the otherwise high-dimensional spectrum.
- Network: a feed-forward neural network maps the vector of peak frequencies {omega_k} (and a few auxiliary features such as the broadband baseline level for nu_en) to the parameters of the n_e(x), nu_en(x) profiles (e.g. profile width, peak density, edge density).
- Training data: synthetic; generated by sweeping the profile parameters, running the forward model, and locating peaks in |Gamma(omega)| with a peak-finder.
- Validation: on held-out synthetic spectra with added Gaussian and frequency-correlated noise; the network is reported to outperform a single-frequency cutoff inversion across the explored profile family.

## Conclusions (as stated by the authors)
- The number and frequency positions of the multi-peaks in the microwave reflection spectrum of an inhomogeneous plasma carry most of the information about the spatial density profile, while peak amplitudes are dominated by collisional damping.
- Feeding only the peak positions to a neural network gives fast, accurate inversion for the n_e(x) profile and the collision frequency, even under noisy conditions where direct full-spectrum fitting becomes unstable.
- The result is positioned as a non-invasive, real-time microwave diagnostic for inhomogeneous low-temperature plasmas such as plasma sheaths around re-entry vehicles, plasma antennas, and processing chamber boundaries.

## Code availability from the authors
None linked. We provide a self-contained reference implementation: a stratified-slab forward model for |Gamma(f)|, a peak finder, and an MLP that maps peak positions to plasma profile parameters.

## Reference code
See `reference_code.py` in this folder. It:
- builds a 1D stratified-slab transfer-matrix forward model for an inhomogeneous cold plasma with prescribed n_e(x) and nu_en(x);
- generates |Gamma(f)| spectra for randomly sampled Gaussian density profiles;
- locates local maxima in |Gamma(f)| as features;
- trains an MLP (peak positions -> profile parameters) and reports the held-out error.

Run: `python reference_code.py` (needs `numpy`, `torch`).
